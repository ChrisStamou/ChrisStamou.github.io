AI Projects

Promotion Prediction for Workforce Planning (Second AI Business Case)

Designed an AI pipeline on synthetic data (5,000 employees) to predict which employees are likely to be promoted within 12 months. The project demonstrates how HR can replace manual, subjective promotion reviews with a data-driven, explainable, and repeatable process. Trained and compared Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost models in Python (scikit-learn, xgboost) with stratified train-test splits, preprocessing of numeric and categorical features, and hyperparameter search. Evaluated with Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1, ROC AUC, and PR AUC. Logistic Regression delivered the best balance of interpretability and predictive performance (ROC AUC ≈ 0.782, Recall ≈ 0.747).
Business Impact: Manual promotion analysis in large firms can take over a month. The AI-assisted approach compresses this to hours, representing an 85–90% time saving per cycle, while improving consistency, transparency, and fairness.
Tools: Python, pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, scikit-learn, xgboost; LaTeX (Overleaf) for report.

Fraud Risk Ranking for Vehicle Insurance (First AI Business Case)

Built a first end-to-end AI prototype on synthetic data (5,000 claims) to rank vehicle insurance claims by fraud risk so investigators can focus on the riskiest subset. Trained and compared Logistic Regression and Random Forest models in Python (scikit-learn) with an 80/20 train-test split, scaling of numeric features and one-hot encoding of categoricals. Evaluated with ROC AUC, Precision, Recall, and F1. Operated the model at a capacity-aligned flag rate (top 25% highest-risk claims) to mirror real investigation constraints.
Results (test set): Logistic Regression AUC 0.898; at 25% flag rate: Precision 0.332, Recall 0.798, F1 0.469 (Random Forest: AUC 0.867; Precision 0.308; Recall 0.750; F1 0.437).
Tools: Python, pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, scikit-learn; LaTeX (Overleaf) for report.
